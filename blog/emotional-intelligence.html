EQ & IQ

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is your ability to understand your emotions, thoughts and feels. It directly related to the inner landscape of your felt sense experience, rather than the logical. Emotions are irrational and sometimes linked to past experiences we are unaware of in our conscious minds. Therefore the better we understand our emotional triggers, and how to recourse ourselves the better we are able to navigate this wild, wild world!

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ is understanding cognitive intelligence and logic (how to get from A to B). EQ is understanding your emotions and inner landscape (how you feel about going from A to B). Back in the day, we had high EQ as we had to be attuned to the wind and the water to survive. Emotional intelligence requires us to feel, listen and be with ~ a skill that has been lost over the millenniums. Now IQ is more widely known and celebrated due it’s ability to make us more productive ($$$$). If we just bulldoze over our EQ, and use our IQ to rationalise our way through life, we’ll be fine right? Clearly not. Hence why EQ is having a comeback!

Why is EQ important?

Ummm. Well, how long do you have? I’m bias but IQ-only living is dull. This is why people make it to the top and then wonder why they are so miserable. EQ is what enables the depth of connection, fulfilment and meaning that we need as humans to live healthy, thriving lives. Life is relationships. Relationship to your partner, colleagues, family, environment, spirit and self. To have functional relationships you need to know yourself intimately. You can’t assert your boundaries if you don’t know what they are. You can't tell someone what you like in the bedroom if you don’t know what you like. You can’t attract what you want in your life if you don’t know what you want. You can be on autopilot ~ lots of people are, but you’ll hit the ceiling quickly and start to seek that primal urge of fulfilment in other (and sometimes destructive) ways.

Mindfulness Reflections

Do you have any biases for or against mindfulness? If so, can you describe them?

I have so much passion for mindfulness! It's been an integral pillar of my wellness journey for about 8 years now. It's an area that I have explored greatly and continue to do so. One thing I will say is that mindfulness is a cognitive based practice. At some point in our journey I believe it's important to bring body-based practices into the fold. Research is showing more and more that the body holds our story. With leaders such as Gabor Mate and Bessel Van Der Kolk it's becoming clear that we need to establish and integrate mindfulness pracices that incorporate the felt sence.

How did you find the overall experience?

Well when I first started it was very confronting, and it set me off on a chain of major life descisions that drastically changed who am I am - in all aspects of my life. It's transformative. I also find it enriching, mindfulness has bought depth, meaning and fulfilment to my life. It's also bought me frustration. At times I wish I didn't swallow the red pill and could go back to ignorance as bliss haha.

What's your daily habit commitment for mindfulness?

Mediation twice a day (morning + night). I do Being meditation which is a version of Vedic meditation.
