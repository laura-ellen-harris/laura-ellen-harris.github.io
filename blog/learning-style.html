My Learning Plan

My long-term career pathway is to be a team leader, mentor or manager in a company that is driving positive change for the planet and it’s people. With my current skillset in leadership, facilitation and team management i feel I’m greatly suited for this.

My strengths when it comes to learning are being driven, reliable and self-responsible. Due to my prior experience of being a business owner I know how to implement structure, routine and supportive aids to assist my learning and productivity.

My weaknesses are that I can rush through things and focus more on efficiency instead of quality.

I’d like to go the extra mile and ensure that what I’m learning is sticking, and that I’m focussing on mastery rather than getting it done quickly.

While at Dev academy i’d like to develop my technical skill in coding, enabling me to find a suitable entry-level position so that I can work towards my greater goals.

While I am working through this program I have committed myself to working full time on Dev Academy and am only seeing 2-3 clients a week (5-6 hours).

I plan to work safely and productively with teams mates and facilitators by try my best to problem solve myself first, making sure I have grounded myself and scoped out the issue before reaching out. I imagine coding can get frustrating, so practicing patience and not letting it mean anything to me on a personal level is important. Moreover, not taking the frustration out on others, especially teachers, is very important! In the theme of leadership, I feel it’s important to be vulnerable and honest while taking responsibility for our emotions and not throwing them around for others to hold.

I’m a lady of leisure, and I’m always committed to finding balance in my work/play life. I recognise that this course is a big learning commitment, and there will be a stressful period. In the past, I have let stress overwhelm me and it can weigh me down emotionally, this time I plan to maintain an “acceptance approach” where it’s okay to feel overwhelmed and to take small breaks when I need to. I’m also mindful of simplifying my life to ensure I have enough space for things that nourish me and keep me resourced such as early nights, healthy food, meditation and exercise.

If I’m struggling, I won’t hesitate to ask for help. I’m a therapist so… I know how valuable support is! Even if it’s talking to a facilitator about what I’m struggling with in the course can be so valuable, as they help to give perspective (and some validation, haha!).

I don’t have set expectations from the facilitation team, but what I’ve discovered so far is that they are professionals that show up and are very generous with their time, while also having boundaries and respect. So my expectation is that they’ll do their best to support students, while also allowing us the opportunity to work through issues on our own and empowering us to find solutions.

In terms of scheduling, I’m going with the ol’ classic 9-5 approach. With that being said, I’m an early riser and I like to ‘get stuck in’. So typically I’ll be up and studying from 730ish-230ish. I like to be flexible, so if I’m needing a longer lunch break i’ll take it etc. I don’t make plans with friend or family during the day as I’m studying.

Thanks for reading!
