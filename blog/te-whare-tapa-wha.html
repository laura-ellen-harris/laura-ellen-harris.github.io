Te Whare Tapa Wellbeing Plan

I LOVE WELLBEING PLANS! I am so here for this.

3 July 2023

Here is my well-being plan for my time at Devacademy. I can go a little OTT, so I’ve created a menu of sorts for each wall. Life changes, weather changes, I’m a woman so my energy levels change throughout the month. It’s vital for me to be able to cater to my cycle and choose an option from a menu that varies in time, energy output, accessibility etc.

- Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing) Gym 5 days a week (before or after school). Exercises can vary and be tailored to which phase of my cycle I am in. When I’m ovulating I can do higher-intensity workouts. When I’m in my luteal phase I like to do yoga and pilates rather than heavy weights.

- Walking my dog along the beach ( I live in Devonport). Connecting to the waters is a crucial part of my overall wellbeing, as well as spending quality time with my dog. Over winter when the days are shorter I may only get one beach walk in a day, and that’s okay.

- Grooming & hygiene - Presentation is important to me and I like to take pride in how look. I don’t mean affluent fashion glam, I’m referring to taking the time to shower, do my hair and wear clothes that represent me.

Taha Hinengaro (mental wellbeing)

- I see a therapist every week. I call it mental hygiene, it’s just as important as going to the dentist.

- Compassion and positive self-talk, especially when things get tough.

- Mediation, twice daily.

- Staying organised by writing lists, using organisational tools and not overcommitting or putting too much on my plate.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

- My spirituality is accessed through embodiment practice and nature.

- Dancing (5 rhythms or ecstatic dance)

- Embodiment practice at home (guided audio)

- Long walks through the bush, hikes in the mountains, and walking along the beach with my dog.

Taha whānau (family wellbeing)

- Checking in with parents on the phone. Having dinners, walks and cups of tea with friends.

- Cooking at home and sharing meals with friends on weeknights. Making plans to do memorable things with friends and family (something to look forward to)

- Deepening connections through intentional intimacy and focussing on loving each other.
