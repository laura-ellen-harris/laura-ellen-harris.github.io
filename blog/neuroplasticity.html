Neuroplasticity and Adopting a Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity:

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity is beneficial as it enables people to take control of their learning and life with an approach that is achievable. If science affirms to us that we can learn anything, or “reprogram” the way our brain thinks and performs, then that in turn fulfils us with a can-do attitude aka self belief.

I like to engage with the principles of neuroplasticity by reconising that I’m in the drivers seat of my learning and life. By building new and robust neural pathways in my brain, I can lean and become competent at anything I set my mind to (with dedication and practice, that is).

Some of ways I can help increase neuroplasticity are by maintaining a sense of newness for my brain ie walking new trails to work, or learning a new skill. Other ways are physical exercise, hydration, and cognitive-based mindfulness tools such as meditation.

A resource that I use regularity for neurplacitiy (information and action) is a neural manifestation platform called TBM. TBM is a hypnosis method that allows you to access root causes for certain neural structures (though patterns and beliefs) and reprogram them with new, more supportive beliefs. It’s amazing!

Growth Mindset:

A growth mindset is a fundamental belief that we are capable of learning, developing and evolving. It’s important to adopt and maintain a growth mindset as it enables us to explore, expand and achieve things in our lifetime. Humans WANT to evolve, it is our nature, although I’d like to add that adopting a growth mindset is not as easy as flicking a switch. Many people experience challenging upbringings, or traumatic events (early childhood as well as adulthood) that in inhibit our sense of self belief, therefore eroding our sense of ability and self esteem.

People who have experienced trauma loose resilience and tend to lean toward a fixed mindset as they don’t have an intrinsic sense of safety or security. For some, making mistakes can feel life threatening and have sever consequences on a nervous system level.

This is also relevant to people who are highly sensitive. If you’d like to learn more, check out this podcast

It’s complex stuff that unique to every individual, so compassion is needed.

In this exploration, what I noticed is how far away I am from my own “practice” of self belief and mindfulness. I mediatte daily and have good health habbits, but life can sometimes become overwhelming and I often fall victim to fixed thinking. In my personal experience, neuroplaciicity and growth mindset is a choice, but to do it well it takes A LOT of consistent awareness, focus and energy.

I’d like to integrate a growth mindset on my journey by giving myself permission to be in an exploration phase, trust that I can adapt and evolve in challenging circumstances and choose to carve out time for reflection, inventory and mindfulness (journall, meditation, play etc). It’s important that I try to let go of control, allow myself to make mistakes, pivot or choose another direction if I want to.
